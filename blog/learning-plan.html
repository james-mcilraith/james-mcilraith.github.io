My learning plan (2nd edition)

Saturday, 28 September 2024

My new learning plan (2nd edition) – looks at some new supplementary learning plan aspects as Foundations comes to a close.

  • What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

What are you hoping to use the skills you gain during this course for? What impact would you like to make once you leave Dev Academy? Do you have a destination in mind?

In addition to having a long-standing interest in wanting to do the course, I mentioned in my earlier learning plan that the course opens up some new possibilities for me. Building my software development know-how alongside other transferable knowledge and skills from the course. And that would feed into and support my social research mahi in new and exciting ways. While the thinking is still a little fuzzy, I am looking forward to utilising the knowledge and skills that I gain to make an impact in the social research field. To be discovered (TBD)!

  • What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Re-evaluate your strengths and limitations from Sprint 2. Has your idea of these changed throughout Foundations? Have you discovered any new ones during this course? How do you think these will help/hinder you during Bootcamp?

I am a keen and steady learner, especially when I am interested in and passionate about a subject. I have reasonably good ‘learning processes’, such as reading, note-taking, and time-management skills. Learning limitations might include software development currency, being side-tracked following up on subjects or by other tasks, and the busyness of everyday life. I would add that the tyranny of distance (from Ōtaki to Wellington) may also prove to be a learning limitation i.e., with the tiredness of commutes.

  • What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I remain a keen and steady learner. And have found myself challenged and excited by the Foundations learning. My learning processes have been reasonably sound. However, I think my biggest concern, perhaps as with others, is the forthcoming Bootcamp’s speed and whether I will be able to keep pace with others or the course, when I have quite an entrenched measured learning style. That learning style is certainly advantageous at times; however, may not suit the more agile sprint format. Or, it may be fine. I remain optimistic.

  • What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

While you will face many technical challenges on Bootcamp, this is not what we would like you to consider in this plan. Non-technical challenges might include staying on top of the material, working in groups, speaking in front of the group, keeping focused for nine weeks, etc.

Building on what was just mentioned, the biggest non-technical challenge I foresee is most probably pair programming or group work. While I welcome the challenge, and the importance of Tuakana-Teina roles, my own pattern of knowledge and skills acquisition tends to be quite independent (at least initially and until I gain a level of proficiency). Where there is pair programming or group work, my learning style pace may be different, and the ‘fast-slow’ of project work may be impacted. It is something that I will remain mindful of (as will my ego).

  • What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

At Dev Academy, we have a significant focus on human skills and improving yourself. Based on your strengths/limitations, what are some areas for development you would like to work on?

For example, this could be communication, teamwork within groups, pairing with others, giving and receiving feedback, developing some leadership skills, or improving your self-awareness and reflective skills.

I am looking forward to working with others – specifically collaborating using the workflows and processes seen in software development. This is a reasonably new way of working for me. And a different and interesting way of getting things done efficiently and effectively for software development purposes. And simply engaging with others and using all the skills mentioned is always helpful.

  • What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

What does support look like to you? Keep in mind, this may not be the same as the Bootcamp team's expectations, but it is helpful to voice these expectations.

My expectations are related to having clear learning (guidance and processes) and learning outcomes (expectations). That these are provided and appropriate support is given to achieve those learning outcomes. And that ultimately I finish the course following a positive learning experience having achieved the learning outcomes. Noting that this relies on me, too!

  • What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

Based on your knowledge of Bootcamp, how and where will you seek help in a timely way?

I am based in the Roa2024 hybrid group. And have been learning where to get help online and through Discord over these past few weeks. I think the in-person catch ups will be helpful. And suit my learning style i.e., bringing my human or technical challenges for a regular meeting to go over them. Noting also, as above, that achieving my learning outcomes is reliant on me putting in the mahi and learning the course material. That’s what I will be focused on and ensuring that I have the right supports and structures in place.

My learning plan (1st edition)

Friday, 06 September 2024

  • What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I am reasonably established in my long term goal or career pathway – as a social researcher working in government for the past decade. That is certainly something that I will be looking to build upon, in due course. This Dev Academy course opens up some new possibilities for me – to build my software development know-how alongside other transferable knowledge and skills from the course. That will hopefully support me to grow my social research career and also chart new directions in the software development field.

  • A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

I am a keen and steady learner, especially when I am interested in and passionate about a subject. I have reasonably good ‘learning processes’, such as reading, note-taking, and time-management skills. Learning limitations might include software development currency, being side-tracked following up on subjects or by other tasks, and the busyness of everyday life. I would add that the tyranny of distance (from Ōtaki to Wellington) may also prove to be a learning limitation i.e., with the tiredness of commutes.

  • What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I think in non-technical / human skills, I am looking forward to working with others in the collaborative software space. Though it may prove challenging at times. The particular form that such collaboration takes is of interest i.e., the software development cycle. An relatedly, how that works in terms of workflow management.

  • A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme?

Working productively and safely: I will be putting in place a structure to look after my wellbeing and work time. For example, spending an appropriate period each day / week looking after wellbeing and ensuring that I am similarly able to (timebox) each sprint appropriately. I will also be looking to ensure positive engagement with course learners and facilitators. For example, managing extraneous communications and engaging fully where needed.

When things are building up: As above, I am also wanting to ensure that my wellbeing is looked after. I have the opportunity to do that working from home and on a hybrid basis. And getting needed exercise and sleep!

  • A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

For Dev Academy course matters, I will seek assistance from my Facilitator, in the first instance. And reach out to fellow learners and facilitators when I am struggling with course-related materials. At home, my partner Sara, is the go to person!

  • A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I expect clear and timely communications from the Facilitation team on what’s needed for the course, whether that is through the Student Handbook, or through other direct communications. Although the course is self-directed, it is still crucial to know the course direction and requirements. Similar clear and timely communications where help is needed on a course problem (tech or human skills). And importantly, supporting a positive collaborative and productive learning environment for everyone.

  • Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

I have mentioned previously that I appreciate the hybrid working model. And would like to ensure that it is maintained as much as possible. Currently, that means working from home on Wednesdays. When I also have family chores to look after.
